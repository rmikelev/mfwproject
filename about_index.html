My Story

Yosemite is a special place for me. My first trip happened when I was 8-years old and my father piled us into his new GM truck and took us camping in the park. That adventure crystalized my joy of the beauty of Yosemite National Park. I travel to Yosemite at least three times a year, enjoying the beauty of Mother Nature, the hiking, biking and the photographic opportunities.

My interest in Photography began when I study Japanese language and culture in Japan. There I bought my first Nikon F camera and captured mostly in black and white the many beautiful sites in Japan. I even developed my first darkroom in our small bathroom processing the images. This love for photography prompted me to get a certificate in commercial photography from UCBerkely. With this experience I spent several year photographing products for agriculture and manufacturing companies.

Eventually, I returned to photographing the beauty of nature, spending time in the West traveling to our national parks.

My Career

When I returned from Japan I continued my University studies and started working at a private girls high school and Santa Monica Community College. From there, went to Fresno State and the following year Merced College where I spent 33 years as a college professor. During the last part of my career I returned to CSU Fresno as an adjunt professor.

During the early years professors had to create their own websites using Dreamweaver. By the time I left Merced College,faculty members were given templates to build their websites. Having left the college and continuing to photograph, using a now defunct program, iWeb, I created my own photographic website that is slowly disintegrating into cyberspace.
